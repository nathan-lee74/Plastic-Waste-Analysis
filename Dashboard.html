Visualizing Plastics: Deliverable from Data

This dashboard presents findings from an analysis of global plastic waste collection data from the Break Free from Plastic (BFFP) audit, covering 2019 and 2020 across 45+ countries.

Research Questions:

  1. Do countries with more volunteers tend to collect more proportions of both high and low density polyethylene plastic in 2019 and 2020?

  2. Do countries with greater forest coverage and more threatened species show a different breakdown of plastic types?

Data Sources:
  • Main dataset: Break Free from Plastic audit data (TidyTuesday)
  • Supplementary dataset: API Ninjas Country API
Key Findings

  • No clear relationship between volunteer count and polyethylene proportion was found, though a general positive trend exists in Figure 1.

  • PET plastic drops dramatically as biodiversity increases — from 71.7% in low biodiversity countries to 21.5% in high biodiversity countries.

  • Threatened species count is the only statistically significant predictor of PET proportion (p = 0.021), explaining 12.5% of variance.

Key Finding

Threatened species count is the only statistically significant predictor of PET plastic proportion (F = 5.841, p = 0.021), explaining 12.5% of variance. As threatened species increases, PET proportion decreases (coefficient = -0.1325).

Forested area showed a similar negative trend (p = 0.079) but was not statistically significant. Together the model explains about 23% of variation in PET proportion, suggesting that ecological richness is meaningfully associated with plastic composition patterns across countries.
